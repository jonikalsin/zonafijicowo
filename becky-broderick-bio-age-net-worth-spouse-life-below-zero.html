Becky Broderick On Life Below Zero: Northern Territories

National Geographic’s Life Below Zero: Northern Territories returned for its second installment in June 2022, and as always it saw 5 faces including its retuning star Becky Broderick, ready for a brand new adventure in the freezing temperature of the Northwest Territories in Canada.

On the show, Becky was often spotted with her collection of sled dogs and pieces of bait fish, all just to provide a warm meal for her family. But it wasn’t just a “meal” that Becky wanted. In the previous season, we’ve seen her craving for animals like Musk Ox, and we’re sure, this season, her craving will even be greater. Meaning, more adventures for us to see.

Furthermore, Becky wasn’t even afraid to take her infant with her to care for her dogs, and this was just the kind of courage the viewers had signed up for. However, she did face some backlash for raising her daughter in the north, but the ones who praised her for it labeled her “the Sue Aikens of Canada”

This survival expert once also had a chat with the Life Below Zero icon Susan Aikens where they talked about breaking gender stereotypes, managing fame, and their close encounters with death.

Joining Becky for the second season of Life Below Zero: Northern Territories were her co-stars Bentley Kakekayash, “Pike” Mike Harrison, Kim Pasche, and Pierre-Yves Duc

The last we checked, Becky had already graced over 16 episodes of Life Below Zero.

Did you know: Becky’s sled dogs, Sadie (black and white, blue eyes) and Max (brown) retired in 2019. She was looking for a new home for her retired dogs then.

How Much Is Becky Broderick Net Worth?

Becky Broderick flaunted a net worth of above $600 thousand in 2022.

Besides being a survival expert, she also worked in the field of mental and wellness. As of 2022, she was working as a “community wellness worker” at the Government of the Northwest Territories for over 10 years. There, her job was to focus on prevention, education, and wellness programming and conduct workshops specializing in the areas of addiction, mental health, and family violence. Also, she acted as a Project Leader for the development and implementation of community-based alcohol and drug treatment program.

Prior to that, Becky worked as an addictions worker at Withdrawal Management Sudbury for 3 months in 2012. And before that, she was a developmental support worker at Simcoe Community Services from Feb 2006 to August 2011.

In case you want to know, a health wellness worker in Canada made around $68 thousand per annum.

As for her education, Becky attended York University from 2005 to 2010 for her specialized honors degree in international development studies in social science. In 2011, she then continued her studies at the same university for her master of arts (disability and health studies) and graduated a year later.

Also, from 2011 to 2012, Becky went to Georgian College for her post-graduate degree in mental health and addiction counseling.

Becky Broderick Spouse

Becky Broderick worked hard, and it was for her spouse Dene Dzen, and their daughter Thi (born in 2018). This survival expert married Dene back in January 2016, even before they could celebrate their first dating anniversary i.e. on September 3, 2016.

Talking more about her husband, Dene Dzen’s real name could be Jake Basil. He did his schooling up to Grade 11 in Lutselk’e and did grade 12 in Yellowknife. After graduating and feeling like he was disconnecting from his homeland, living in Whitehorse, Dene and Becky then moved back to Lutselk’e.

According to Becky, it was also because she wanted to raise her daughter to be “a self-sufficient badass” that she chose to come back to Lutselk’e. But the hardest thing about living there was darkness. “It’s dark all the time. So I had to learn to do everything in the dark. Get water in the dark. Chop wood in the dark. Travel in the dark,” she explained.
