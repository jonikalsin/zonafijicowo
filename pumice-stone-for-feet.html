What to Look for in a Pumice Stone

Pore size

Depending upon the use, the size of pores on your preferred pumice stone may differ. For someone who has hard calluses that have built up over a long time, you would need a stone with large pores. However, if you need something to smoothen out cracked heels and have sensitive skin, it would do you much better to opt for something with smaller pores as they are much easier on your skin. The preferred pore size boils down to what you expect to get done with your pumice stone for feet.

Shape

The shape of the pumice might appear trivial; however, it’s much more important than that. The correct shape and size of the pumice stone can lead to a much more comfortable time using it for your feet. Besides, the product's utility is directly dependent on your ability to use it properly. And, to use a pumice stone properly, you need it to be in an ergonomic shape to maintain a solid grip.

Material

Like any other product you have ever bought, the material of the pumice stone you are using for your feet is an important consideration. This is because some materials might be a little harsher than others, but necessary to get rid of tough calluses where the user has thicker skin. In other cases, some synthetic materials might even trigger an allergic response. So make sure to put in the appropriate amount of research on the material of your product before actually committing to a purchase. This will allow you to get the highest value for your money!

Additional features

Although there isn’t much you can do to add value to pumice stones, there are manufacturers who go the extra mile and provide additional comfort and accessibility by adding handles and loops to the stone. Before you make a purchase, find out if there is any other product that provides the same utility but with additional features.
