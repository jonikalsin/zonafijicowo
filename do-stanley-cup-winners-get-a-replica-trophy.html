Do Stanley Cup winners get a replica trophy?

July 01, 2024
blog

The Stanley Cup is one of the most prestigious trophies in professional sports, awarded annually to the National Hockey League (NHL) champion.

As hockey fans around the world celebrate the crowning of a new champion each year, many wonder whether the winning team and its players are presented with a replica of the iconic Stanley Cup.

The Original Stanley Cup

The original Stanley Cup, often referred to as the "Dominion Hockey Challenge Cup," was first awarded in 1893 to recognize the top amateur ice hockey club in Canada. It was donated by Sir Frederick Arthur Stanley, the 16th Earl of Derby, and quickly became the symbol of excellence in the world of professional hockey.

The original Cup, which is now displayed in the vault room of the Hockey Hall of Fame in Toronto, is not presented to the winning team but is instead kept in the Hall of Fame for public viewing.

The Presentation of the Stanley Cup

When a team wins the Stanley Cup, they are not awarded a replica of the original trophy. Instead, the winning team is presented with the actual Stanley Cup, a tradition that dates back to the early years of the NHL.

The Cup is engraved with the names of the players, coaches, and management of the championship team, creating a lasting record of their achievement. This longstanding tradition adds to the allure and prestige of the Stanley Cup, as each winning team's name is immortalized on the trophy itself.

Replica Trophies and Commemorative Items

While the winning team does not receive a replica Stanley Cup, they are often provided with other commemorative items to celebrate their victory. These items may include championship rings, plaques, and other memorabilia that serve as tangible reminders of their historic achievement.

Also, the NHL and the winning team often collaborate on the production of merchandise, allowing fans to purchase replica trophies and other collectibles to commemorate the championship win.

The Legacy of the Stanley Cup

The Stanley Cup holds a special place in the hearts of hockey enthusiasts and players alike. Its rich history and the tradition of engraving the names of champions make it a unique and revered symbol of excellence in professional sports.

The absence of a replica trophy for the winning team further enhances the mystique and prestige of the Stanley Cup, reinforcing its status as one of the most coveted trophies in all of sports.

 

While the winning team of the Stanley Cup does not receive a replica trophy, they are honored with the presentation of the original Stanley Cup, a tradition that has endured for over a century.

The trophy's legacy, combined with the commemorative items and merchandise associated with the championship win, ensures that the triumph is celebrated and remembered for years to come.
