Andes Plane Crash Rescuer Sergio Catalan Died In 2020 

As said earlier, Sergio Catalan made headlines when he got linked to the Andes Plane crash that happened on October 13, 1972. 

On December 21, 1972, while herding cattle with his sons, including Juan de la Cruz, near the Barroso River, he noticed someone waving from the opposite bank. It was Fernando Parrado and Roberto Canessa.

Unable to communicate through screams due to the noisy current, Sergio tossed a notebook and pen to those two people. 

Roberto and Fernando shared crash details, and seeing their condition, Catalan responded with four loaves of bread and an encouraging message.

Following that, Sergio did everything to help the plane crash survivors. Sadly, Catalan took his last breath on February 11, 2020. His remains were buried in the Parque San Fernando cemetery.
