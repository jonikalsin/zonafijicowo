Sarkodie Bio, Age, Net Worth, Profession

June 03, 2024
blog

Sarkodie, real name Michael Owusu Addo, is a rapper and businessman from Ghana. He is from Tema and has significantly impacted Ghana’s music scene.

Sarkodie was the first recipient of BET’s Best International Flow Act, among many other accolades. He sees himself as a leading advocate for the Azonto genre and dance.

What is the Net Worth of Sarkodie? Salary, Earnings

As of 2023, Sarkodie’s net worth is anticipated to be $12 million. Sarkodie makes money in a number of ways. As a committed businessman, he is the owner of the Sark by Yas clothing line. He launched it on April 27, 2013, featuring men’s, women’s, and kids’ accessories.

At the University of Ghana, Legon, he was chosen to represent Samsung Electronics as a brand ambassador in 2012. Sarkodie’s backgrounds and signature were added to Samsung’s Galaxy Pocket and Chief Hero phones during the unveiling ceremony, and users could download exclusive Sarkodie music.

He also agreed to a contract with “FanMilk Ghana” in 2013. Sarkodie worked with Tigo Telecommunications Ghana, who funded his Rapperholic tour, to promote its brand. He was appointed an ambassador for the ‘Year of Return’ campaign by the Creative Arts Industry Forum on October 30, 2019.

Where was Sarkodie born? Ethnicity, Nationality, Family, Education

Sarkodie was the fourth child of his mother, Emma Maame Aggrey, and was born on July 10, 1085, in Tema, Ghana. He earned a Western African Senior School Certificate from Tema Methodist Senior School and a Graphic Design degree from IPMC. He was an underground rapper during his high school years until he met his manager Duncan Williams in the renowned rap tournament Kasahare Level.

How did Sarkodie start his Professional Career?

Before meeting his former manager Duncan Williams of Duncwills Entertainment while competing in Adom FM’s rap competition Kasahare Level, Sarkodie started out as an underground rapper. He also met other performers at this occasion, including Edem and Castro, who introduced him to Hammer of the Last Two, a renowned producer who was taken by his rapping style. Sarkodie’s album Volta Regime was recorded with help from Hammer.

He worked with Hammer on his self-titled debut album. On this album, R2Bees members Kwaw Kese, J-Town, Sway, and Paedae all made cameo performances. Both critics and fans gave “Makye” high marks. Sarkodie’s songs Push and Baby were voted ninth and nineteenth on Joy FM’s list of the Top 50 Songs of 2009. ‘Baby,’ the album’s lead single, became a hit and increased his exposure.

His second album, “Rapperholic,” which was published in 2012, featured five singles: “Good Bye,” “One Time for Your Mind,” “You Go Kill Me,” “Living Legend,” and “Onyame Nhyira.” He undertook a cross-country promotional tour for the album, which resulted in his first BET nomination and subsequent victory. Other albums include ‘Adonai’ from 2014, ‘New Guy’ and ‘Mary’ from 2015, ‘Highest’ from 2017, and ‘Alfa’ from 2019.
